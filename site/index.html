About

On March 28, a 7.7 magnitude earthquake devastated Myanmar's second-largest city, Mandalay. Whole apartment blocks crumbled like chalk, ancient temples in Bagan were shattered, stone stupas and golden pagodas turned to rubble. Monasteries collapsed. Monks buried. What's gone isn't just lives. It's centuries of memory, spirit, prayer. and no one’s coming to help. the official death count is low, but that’s only because there’s no one left to count. locals are saying it could be tens of thousands.


Myanmar was already bleeding. In 2021, the military (led by dog bastard Min Aung Hlaing) overthrew the democratic government. It's been pure violence since: burned villages, mass arrests, airstrikes on children. The people didn't give up; they formed the National Unity Government (NUG) to hold the line with grit and jungle and hope.


In the ruins of the earthquake, while people are sobbing over the dead, Min Aung Hlaing continued bombing. That's why we're throwing a Milady charity auction: every bid helps survivors and supports the people who refused to bow. Courage is our favorite virtue.

1Countdown

The charity auction will officially begin at 5:00pm Eastern Time (UTC -4) on Sunday, April 6th. When the countdown reaches zero, the one-week donation period begins.

2Auction Week

For one week, you can make donations through this website. Every donation counts towards both the auction and the raffle. Bids of 0.5 ETH or greater within the final 30 minutes of the auction extend the auction by 30 minutes.

3Winner

When the auction ends, the donor who contributed the highest amount will automatically win the auction prize.

4Raffle

When the auction ends, a secure random raffle contract will automatically give the winner their grand prize. Your chances of winning are proportional to your total donation amount. The more you donate, the better your chances of winning!

All donations from this auction and raffle will be trustlessly routed through Myanmar Earthquake Relief, a fund of Friends of Pariaman (an American 501(c)(3) registered non-profit) to local, non-dictatorship earthquake relief efforts in Myanmar. This is a group of Burmese diaspora that we personally trust.
